My name is Louis Page and this website was made to document my adventures in 3D printing.

The printer I use is called the Prusa i3 MK3. It is an FDM type 3D Printer. This means that it uses a hot extruder to extrude plastic special thrermoplastic, refered to as filament, onto the print bed along its X, Y, and Z axis. (Pictured on the right)

Something that makes this model of printer unique is that it is assembled using 3D printed parts. All of the orange on the printer is printed out using other printers of the same model at the factory. Prusa Research utilizes their design and stands behind it enough to rely on these printers to produce all future printers that they sell. This brings about the idea of a "child 3D printer", having the ability to build multiple 3D printers from one original printer.

The idea of the "child printer" illustrates the unique problems that can be solved by incorporating a 3D printer into your workflow. New possibilities present themselves when you have the ability to create small plastic components at will. Small repairs that would otherwise be impossible due to lack of parts are now possible.

The future of 3D printing is very bright. As an ever growing industry, 3D printing will shift from a hobby to a necessity. Every home will have a 3D printer to print whatever is needed. Going to the store for the one part you need to complete your project will be a thing of the past. The future looks bright thanks to the development of the 3D printer.
